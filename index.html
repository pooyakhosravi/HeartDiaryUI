    Our Purpose
    The advent of biometric technology and sensors has allowed users to become more aware of their exercise and health habits. Wearable devices give a plethora of biometrics, including heart rate, step count, and surface body temperature. Our goal is to take these biometrics and find geospatial patterns that will assist our users in making more mindful health decisions, and offer recommendations for activities that they will find relaxing. Variance in resting heart rate depends on a number of factors. Outside of genetics, disease, drugs, and exercise, human emotion and stress are the largest actors on resting heart rate, and stress is in high supply for those in academia.

    Our goal is to take the data that is available through the biometric sensors (primarily heart rate and skin temperature, as well as step count) and relate it to key environmental factors, such as location, weather, and ambient sound (in decibels) to determine stressful moments in a person’s day, chronic stress throughout an extended period of time, and an overall increase in resting heart rate.Our application aims to increase the mindfulness of our users to their stress levels, and recommend them ways to relax. Ultimately, we hope that our application will raise the awareness of the user’s physical and mental health, and how stress affects their overall health.
